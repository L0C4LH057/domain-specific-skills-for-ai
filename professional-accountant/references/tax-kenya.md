## 1. Corporation Tax (CIT)

**Governing Law:** Income Tax Act Cap 470

### Rates:
| Entity Type | Rate |
|---|---|
| Resident companies (general) | 30% |
| Non-resident companies | 37.5% |
| Newly listed companies (NSE, first 5 years) | 25% |
| Export Processing Zone (EPZ) enterprises | 0% (first 10 years), 25% (next 10 years) |
| Small & Micro Enterprises (annual turnover < KES 1M) | 1% turnover tax (flat) |

### Key features:
- Tax year follows company's accounting year
- **Instalment Tax**: paid quarterly — 25% of estimated tax each quarter
  - Instalments due: 20th of 4th, 6th, 9th, and 12th months of financial year
- **Balance of Tax**: due within **4 months** after financial year end
- Annual return: due **within 6 months** after financial year end

### Allowable deductions:
✅ Business expenses wholly and exclusively for production of income
✅ Interest on loans (thin cap: related-party debt/equity ≤ 3:1)
✅ Donations to approved charitable organizations (up to 1% of turnover)
✅ Wear and tear deductions (equivalent of depreciation for tax)
✅ Investment deduction (100% for industrial buildings, 150% for EPZ)

### Wear and Tear Deductions (Tax Depreciation):
| Asset Class | Rate |
|---|---|
| Industrial buildings | 10% on cost |
| Commercial buildings | 10% on cost |
| Computer & peripherals | 30% on WDV |
| Motor vehicles (general) | 25% on WDV |
| Heavy vehicles & machinery | 25% on WDV |
| Furniture & fittings | 12.5% on WDV |
| Other plant & machinery | 12.5% on WDV |

WDV = Written Down Value (reducing balance basis)

## 2. Value Added Tax (VAT)

**Governing Law:** VAT Act 2013 (as amended)

### Rate: **16%** (standard rate)

### Registration:
- Mandatory when taxable supplies exceed **KES 5 million** per year
- Voluntary registration permitted below threshold

### Key categories:
| Category | Examples | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Standard-rated | Most goods & services | 16% VAT charged |
| Zero-rated | Exports, basic food items, medical supplies | 0% (input VAT refundable) |
| Exempt | Educational services, financial services, insurance | No VAT charged or claimed |

### Zero-rated goods include:
- Milk, butter, ghee, vegetable cooking fat
- Bread (not confectionery)
- Medicaments, medical equipment
- Maize flour (household size ≤ 2kg)
- Agricultural inputs (seeds, fertilizers, pesticides for crops)
- Exported goods and services

### Exempt supplies include:
- Financial services (excluding discretionary fund management)
- Insurance services
- Gambling and gaming
- Unprocessed foodstuffs (raw agricultural produce)

### Filing and remittance:
- **Monthly** VAT returns via iTax — due **20th of the following month**
- Penalty for late payment: 5% of unpaid tax + 1% interest per month
- **TIMS (Tax Invoice Management System)**: All VAT-registered taxpayers must
  use TIMS-compliant ETRs (Electronic Tax Registers) or eTIMS (electronic)
  — mandatory since November 2022

### Digital Services:
- Non-resident companies providing digital services must register for **Simplified VAT**
- No physical presence required — register via KRA simplified regime

## 3. PAYE — Pay As You Earn

**Governing Law:** Income Tax Act Cap 470, Section 37

### Individual Tax Bands (2024):
| Monthly Income (KES) | Annual Income (KES) | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 24,000 | 0 – 288,000 | 10% |
| 24,001 – 32,333 | 288,001 – 388,000 | 25% |
| 32,334 – 500,000 | 388,001 – 6,000,000 | 30% |
| 500,001 – 800,000 | 6,000,001 – 9,600,000 | 32.5% |
| Above 800,000 | Above 9,600,000 | 35% |

### Personal Relief:
- **KES 2,400 per month** (KES 28,800 per year) — deducted from tax liability
- **Insurance Relief**: 15% of premiums paid for life insurance, education policy (max KES 60,000/yr)
- **Disability Exemption**: First KES 150,000 per month if disabled

### Tax-exempt benefits:
- Medical cover (up to KES 1 million per year for employee + family — Finance Act 2023)
- Employer pension contribution (up to KES 30,000 per month — deductible for employee)
- Per diem allowances (up to KES 2,000 per day within Kenya — Finance Act 2022)
- Bonuses, overtime up to KES 12,500/month for low-income earners

### PAYE remittance:
- Deduct from salary on payday
- **Remit to KRA by 9th of the following month** via iTax
- Annual PAYE reconciliation return due 31 January
- P9 forms (employee tax certificate) — issue to all employees by 28 February

## 4. Withholding Tax (WHT)

### Key WHT rates:
| Payment Type | Resident | Non-Resident |
|---|---|---|
| Dividends | 5% | 10% |
| Interest (bank) | 15% | 15% |
| Interest (other) | 15% | 15% |
| Rent | 10% (residential 7.5%) | 30% |
| Royalties | 5% | 20% |
| Management fees | 5% | 20% |
| Professional fees | 5% | 20% |
| Construction contracts | 3% | 20% |
| Director fees | 15% | 20% |
| Commission | 5% | 20% |

### Remittance:
- **Remit to KRA by 20th of the following month**
- File WHT return on iTax
- Issue WHT certificate (KRA form) to recipient within 30 days

## 5. Statutory Deductions (Employer Obligations)

Every employer must deduct and remit these in addition to PAYE:

### NSSF — National Social Security Fund (New Act, implemented 2024):
| Tier | Employee Contribution | Employer Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Tier I (first KES 7,000 of gross) | 6% (KES 420 max) | 6% (KES 420 max) |
| Tier II (earnings above Tier I) | 6% | 6% |
- **Maximum employee contribution**: 6% of gross salary (no cap under new Act)
- Remit by **9th of following month** via NSSF portal

### NHIF → SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund — from October 2024):
- **Rate**: 2.75% of gross salary (replaces fixed NHIF bands)
- No maximum cap
- **Employer does NOT contribute** (employee-only deduction)
- Remit by **9th of following month**

### Housing Levy (Affordable Housing Levy — Finance Act 2023):
- **Employee contribution**: 1.5% of gross salary
- **Employer contribution**: 1.5% of gross salary (additional cost to employer)
- Remit by **9th of following month** via KRA

## 6. Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

- **Rate**: 15% on net gains from transfer of property
- **Effective 2023**: Rate increased from 5% to 15% (Finance Act 2022)
- Applies to: sale of land, buildings, shares (unlisted companies)
- **Exempt**: shares listed on Nairobi Securities Exchange
- **Principal Private Residence exemption**: gain on sale of main home — exempt
- Due date: within **30 days** of transfer

---

## 7. Turnover Tax (TOT)

- For businesses with annual turnover between **KES 1 million and KES 50 million**
- Rate: **3%** of gross sales (no deductions allowed)
- Cannot also be registered for VAT if using TOT
- Pay monthly via iTax by **20th of following month**
- Simple alternative to CIT for small businesses

## 8. Excise Duty

Applies to specific goods and services. Key rates:
| Item | Rate |
|---|---|
| Beer and stout | KES 134.22 per litre |
| Spirits | KES 335.30 per litre |
| Cigarettes | KES 4,805 per 1,000 sticks |
| Telephone services | 15% of fees |
| Fees charged by banks | 20% of fees |
| Imported goods (various) | 10–25% |
| Betting, gaming | 7.5% of revenue |

## 10. Tax Incentives & Exemptions (Kenya)

| Incentive | Detail |
|---|---|
| Special Economic Zones (SEZ) | 10% CIT (first 10 years), 15% thereafter |
| EPZ enterprises | 0% CIT (first 10 years) |
| NSE-listed companies (new listings) | 25% CIT (first 5 years) |
| Small businesses (turnover < KES 1M) | 0% (exempt from all income tax) |
| Approved pension funds | Exempt from income tax |
| Investment Deduction | 100% on new industrial buildings; 150% in EPZ |
