# Audit Preparation Reference

## Types of Audits

| Type | Who Conducts | Purpose | Mandatory For |
|------|-------------|---------|---------------|
| Statutory External Audit | Licensed firm (ICAN, ACCA, CPA) | Independent opinion on financial statements | PLCs, banks, large companies, NGOs with donor requirements |
| Internal Audit | Internal auditors or outsourced | Risk management, controls review | Large companies; good practice for SMEs |
| Tax Audit | Tax authority (FIRS, GRA, KRA, SARS, HMRC, IRS) | Verify tax compliance and accuracy | Any business (risk-based selection) |
| Due Diligence | Buyer's accountants | Verify financial position before acquisition | M&A transactions |
| Donor/Grant Audit | Donor-specified auditors | Verify use of funds | NGOs, project-based funding |

## Preparing for a Statutory Audit

### 3 Months Before Year-End
- [ ] Review trial balance — identify and resolve unusual balances
- [ ] Clear old reconciling items from bank reconciliations
- [ ] Physically count and value all inventory
- [ ] Review fixed asset register — dispose of/write off obsolete assets
- [ ] Collect all outstanding debtors — or provision for bad debts
- [ ] Ensure all accruals are captured for the year
- [ ] Review and pay all outstanding statutory liabilities (VAT, PAYE, pension)

### Documents Auditors Will Request (Standard List)

**Company & Governance**
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Memorandum & Articles of Association
- Board resolutions for the year
- Register of directors, shareholders, charges

**Accounting Records**
- Trial balance (at year-end and at audit fieldwork date)
- General ledger for the full year
- All bank statements for the year + bank reconciliations
- Cash book / petty cash records
- Fixed asset register with depreciation schedule

**Revenue & Receivables**
- Sales invoices (sample; auditors will select)
- Accounts receivable aging at year-end
- Customer confirmations (auditors may send circularisation letters)
- Deferred revenue schedule (if any)
- Revenue contracts or agreements

**Purchases & Payables**
- Purchase invoices (sample)
- Accounts payable aging at year-end
- Supplier statement reconciliations
- Goods received notes / delivery notes

**Payroll**
- Payroll schedules for all 12 months
- Staff list at year-start and year-end with salary details
- PAYE computations and payment receipts
- Pension remittance schedules
- HR contracts for new joiners and termination letters for leavers

**Tax Compliance**
- VAT returns filed and payment receipts for the year
- PAYE monthly returns and remittance receipts
- CIT returns (prior year) and current year computations
- WHT schedules and credit notes received
- Tax clearance certificate (if applicable)
- Any correspondence with tax authority

**Banking & Financing**
- Bank statements for all accounts
- Bank reconciliation statements (month by month)
- Loan agreements and amortisation schedules
- Investment account statements
- Director loan account statements

**Inventory & Fixed Assets**
- Inventory count sheets (signed by counters and supervisors)
- Inventory valuation at cost or NRV (lower of)
- Fixed asset schedule (additions, disposals, depreciation)
- Insurance schedule covering fixed assets

## Preparing for a Tax Audit (FIRS, KRA, SARS, etc.)

### Know Your Rights
- Tax authority must give written notice before an audit (in most jurisdictions)
- You have the right to be represented by a tax consultant or accountant
- Respond to all queries in writing, within stated deadlines
- Keep copies of everything submitted

### Documents to Organize
1. **Tax returns filed**: All CIT, VAT, PAYE returns for the period under review
2. **Payment receipts**: Evidence of all tax payments (keep receipts forever)
3. **Books of account**: Accounting records supporting all figures in returns
4. **Sales evidence**: Invoices, contracts, bank receipts for revenue
5. **Expense evidence**: Invoices, receipts, contracts for claimed deductions
6. **WHT certificates**: Received from customers/payers
7. **Import/export documents**: For businesses with international trade
8. **Transfer pricing documentation**: For group companies/international transactions

### Common Tax Audit Triggers
- Large year-on-year revenue swings without explanation
- Low effective tax rate relative to industry
- High expenses as % of revenue
- Industry-specific triggers (e.g., cash-heavy businesses)
- Inconsistency between VAT returns and CIT returns
- Tip-offs or third-party information
- Failure to file returns (automatic audit)

### Responding to Assessment
If tax authority raises an additional assessment:
1. Review thoroughly — errors are common
2. Request detailed breakdown if not provided
3. Within objection period (typically 30–90 days), file formal objection if disputed
4. Gather evidence to support your position
5. Negotiate where appropriate (tax authorities often accept settlements)
6. If unresolved, escalate to Tax Appeal Tribunal (Nigeria), Tax Appeals Tribunal (Kenya), Tax Court (South Africa), First-tier Tribunal (UK), Tax Court (US)

## Audit Opinion Types

| Opinion | Meaning | Action Required |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Unqualified ("Clean") | Financial statements present fairly in all material respects | No action |
| Qualified | Financial statements present fairly, except for [specific matter] | Address the exception |
| Adverse | Financial statements do NOT present fairly | Major restatement needed |
| Disclaimer | Auditor cannot form an opinion (insufficient evidence) | Provide records; fix record-keeping |
| Emphasis of Matter | Clean opinion but attention drawn to something significant (e.g., going concern) | Address the highlighted issue |

## Going Concern Assessment

Auditors must assess whether the company can continue operating for at least 12 months. Red flags that trigger going concern doubt:

- Net current liabilities (current liabilities > current assets)
- Net liabilities overall (total liabilities > total assets)
- Negative operating cash flows for multiple periods
- Loan covenant breaches or upcoming maturities that cannot be refinanced
- Loss of major customer or contract
- Significant pending legal proceedings
- Inability to pay statutory liabilities on time
- Dependence on single supplier who may fail

**Management response** (to include in financial statements notes):
- Management's assessment of the situation
- Mitigating actions (refinancing plan, cost reduction, new sales pipeline)
- Cash flow forecasts demonstrating viability
- Letter of support from parent company or shareholders (if available)
