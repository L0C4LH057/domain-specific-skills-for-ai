# Tax by Country Reference Guide

## NIGERIA

### Governing Bodies & Laws
- **FIRS** (Federal Inland Revenue Service) — federal taxes
- **State IRS** — state taxes (PAYE, consumption tax)
- **CAMA 2020** — Companies and Allied Matters Act (corporate governance)
- **PITA** — Personal Income Tax Act (Cap P8 LFN 2004, as amended)
- **CITA** — Companies Income Tax Act (Cap C21 LFN 2004, as amended)
- **VATA** — Value Added Tax Act (as amended by Finance Acts 2019–2023)
- **Finance Acts**: 2019, 2020, 2021, 2023 — major reforms, always check latest

### Corporate Income Tax (CIT)
| Company Type | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| Large companies (turnover > ₦100m) | 30% |
| Medium companies (₦25m–₦100m turnover) | 20% |
| Small companies (turnover < ₦25m) | 0% (exempt) |
| Agricultural companies (1st 5 years) | 0% |
| Export-oriented companies | Reduced rates possible |

**Filing**: CIT return due 6 months after year-end. Estimated tax (installments) paid quarterly.

### Value Added Tax (VAT)
- **Standard Rate**: 7.5% (increased from 5% by Finance Act 2020)
- **Exempt supplies**: Basic food items, medical services, educational materials, exported services, baby products, agricultural equipment
- **Zero-rated**: Exported goods and services
- **Registration threshold**: Any business making taxable supplies
- **Filing**: Monthly VAT returns (Form 002) due 21st of following month
- **Withholding VAT**: Government MDAs, large companies, and banks must withhold VAT at source

### PAYE (Pay As You Earn) — Personal Income Tax
```
Gross Income Computation:
  Basic + All Allowances = Gross Income

Statutory Deductions (before CRA):
  - Pension: 8% of (Basic + Housing + Transport) — employee contribution
  - NHF (National Housing Fund): 2.5% of basic salary
  - NHIS (National Health Insurance): 5% of basic salary (where applicable)
  - Life Assurance premiums (actual)

CRA (Consolidated Relief Allowance):
  = ₦200,000 + 20% of Gross Income
  (whichever is higher)

Chargeable Income = Gross Income − Statutory Deductions − CRA − Other Reliefs

Tax Rates (graduated):
  First ₦300,000     →  7%
  Next ₦300,000      →  11%
  Next ₦500,000      →  15%
  Next ₦500,000      →  19%
  Next ₦1,600,000    →  21%
  Above ₦3,200,000   →  24%
```

**Filing**: Employer files annual PAYE return by January 31. Monthly remittance by 10th of following month to State IRS.

**Minimum Income Tax**: If PAYE < 1% of gross income, minimum tax of 1% applies (with exceptions).

### Withholding Tax (WHT)
| Payment Type | WHT Rate (Company) | WHT Rate (Individual) |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Dividends | 10% | 10% |
| Interest | 10% | 10% |
| Rent | 10% | 10% |
| Director fees | 10% | 10% |
| Consultancy/professional fees | 10% | 5% |
| Construction contracts | 5% | 5% |
| Technical fees | 10% | 5% |
| Commissions | 10% | 5% |

WHT is a tax credit — deducted at source, offset against final tax liability. File WHT returns monthly.

### Tax Exemptions & Incentives (Nigeria)
- **Pioneer Status**: Full CIT exemption 3–5 years for qualifying industries (apply to NIPC)
- **Export Expansion Grant (EEG)**: Rebate for non-oil exporters
- **Free Trade Zones**: Exempt from most federal taxes (operate under NEPZA)
- **Agricultural sector**: Reduced rates, exemptions on farm equipment (import duty)
- **Small company exemption**: <₦25m turnover = 0% CIT
- **Research & Development**: Weighted deductions available
- **Graduate employment incentive**: Additional deduction for employing fresh graduates

## GHANA

### Governing Bodies & Laws
- **GRA** (Ghana Revenue Authority)
- **Income Tax Act, 2015 (Act 896)**
- **VAT Act, 2013 (Act 870)** + amendments

### Corporate Income Tax
- Standard rate: **25%**
- Manufacturing (outside Accra/Tema): **18.75%**
- Financial institutions: **25%**
- Mining: **35%**
- Free Zone enterprises: **15%** (after 10-year holiday)
- Non-traditional exports: **0%** (up to 10 years)

### VAT (Ghana)
- VAT rate: **15%**
- NHIL (National Health Insurance Levy): **2.5%**
- GETFund Levy: **2.5%**
- COVID-19 Levy: **1%** (check current status)
- Flat Rate VAT for retailers: **3% on gross value**
- Threshold: GHS 200,000 annual turnover

### Personal Income Tax (Ghana)
```
Annual Chargeable Income → Rate:
First GHS 4,380        →  0%
Next GHS 1,320         →  5%
Next GHS 1,560         →  10%
Next GHS 38,000        →  17.5%
Next GHS 192,000       →  25%
Above GHS 240,000      →  30%
```

## KENYA

### Governing Bodies & Laws
- **KRA** (Kenya Revenue Authority)
- **Income Tax Act (Cap 470)**
- **VAT Act, 2013**
- **Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 2020**

### Corporate Tax (Kenya)
- Resident companies: **30%**
- Non-resident companies: **37.5%**
- Newly listed companies on NSE: **25%** (5 years)
- Export Processing Zone (EPZ): **0%** (10 years), then **25%**
- Special Economic Zone (SEZ): **10%** (10 years), then **15%**

### VAT (Kenya)
- Standard rate: **16%**
- Zero-rated: Exports, specified supplies
- Exempt: Educational services, financial services, medical services
- Registration threshold: KES 5 million annual turnover

### PAYE (Kenya)
```
Monthly Taxable Pay → Rate:
Up to KES 24,000        →  10%
KES 24,001–32,333       →  25%
KES 32,334–500,000      →  30%
KES 500,001–800,000     →  32.5%
Above KES 800,000       →  35%

Personal Relief: KES 2,400/month
Insurance Relief: 15% of premium paid (max KES 5,000/month)
```

### NHIF & NSSF (Kenya)
- NHIF: Graduated scale based on gross pay (employee contribution)
- NSSF (new rates 2023): Tier I = 6% (max KES 360), Tier II = 6% on excess (employer matches)

## SOUTH AFRICA

### Governing Bodies & Laws
- **SARS** (South African Revenue Service)
- **Income Tax Act 58 of 1962**
- **VAT Act 89 of 1991**
- **Companies Act 71 of 2008**

### Corporate Income Tax
- Standard rate: **27%** (reduced from 28% effective 1 April 2022)
- Small Business Corporation (SBC): Graduated rates
  - R0–R95,750: 0%
  - R95,751–R365,000: 7%
  - R365,001–R550,000: 21%
  - Above R550,000: 27%

### VAT (South Africa)
- Standard rate: **15%**
- Zero-rated: Basic foodstuffs (brown bread, milk, eggs, etc.), exported goods, international transport
- Exempt: Residential rental, educational services, public transport
- Threshold: R1 million annual turnover (voluntary from R50,000)

### PAYE (South Africa) — Tax Year March to February
Individual tax rates (2024/25):
```
R1–R237,100             →  18%
R237,101–R370,500       →  26%
R370,501–R512,800       →  31%
R512,801–R673,000       →  36%
R673,001–R857,900       →  39%
R857,901–R1,817,000     →  41%
Above R1,817,000        →  45%

Primary Rebate: R17,235/year
Secondary Rebate (age 65+): R9,444/year
```

### Provisional Tax (South Africa)
Self-employed and companies pay two provisional tax payments during the year, with a third (top-up) payment option after year-end.

## UNITED KINGDOM

### Governing Bodies & Laws
- **HMRC** (His Majesty's Revenue & Customs)
- **Corporation Tax Act 2010**
- **Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003 (ITEPA)**
- **Value Added Tax Act 1994**
- **Making Tax Digital (MTD)** — mandatory digital filing

### Corporation Tax (UK)
- Small profits rate (≤£50,000 profit): **19%**
- Main rate (>£250,000 profit): **25%** (effective April 2023)
- Marginal relief: Applies between £50,000–£250,000 (effective rate tapered)

### VAT (UK)
- Standard rate: **20%**
- Reduced rate: **5%** (domestic fuel, children's car seats, etc.)
- Zero-rated: Food, children's clothing, books, new residential construction
- Exempt: Financial services, insurance, education, healthcare
- Registration threshold: £90,000 rolling 12-month turnover (2024)
- MTD for VAT: Mandatory digital record-keeping and quarterly filing via compatible software

### PAYE & NI (UK) — Tax Year April to March
```
Income Tax (2024/25):
Personal Allowance: £12,570 (0%)
Basic Rate: £12,571–£50,270  →  20%
Higher Rate: £50,271–£125,140 →  40%
Additional Rate: Above £125,140 →  45%

National Insurance (Employee):
Primary Threshold–Upper Earnings Limit: 8%
Above Upper Earnings Limit: 2%

Employer NI: 13.8% above Secondary Threshold
```

### Key UK Exemptions & Reliefs
- **Annual Investment Allowance (AIA)**: 100% deduction up to £1m on plant/machinery
- **R&D Tax Credits**: SMEs 86% enhancement (or 10% cash credit); RDEC 20%
- **Entrepreneurs' Relief (Business Asset Disposal Relief)**: 10% CGT on first £1m lifetime gains
- **Small Business Rate Relief**: For business properties with rateable value < £12,000
- **Employment Allowance**: Up to £5,000 reduction in employer NI per year

## UNITED STATES

### Governing Bodies
- **IRS** (Internal Revenue Service) — federal
- **State Departments of Revenue** — state taxes (vary widely)

### Federal Corporate Tax
- Flat rate: **21%** (Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017)
- Pass-through entities (LLCs, S-Corps, partnerships): Income taxed at owner level
- Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT): Applies to large corporations (15% book minimum)

### Federal Individual Income Tax (2024)
```
$0–$11,600          →  10%  (Single)
$11,601–$47,150     →  12%
$47,151–$100,525    →  22%
$100,526–$191,950   →  24%
$191,951–$243,725   →  32%
$243,726–$609,350   →  35%
Above $609,350      →  37%
```
(Married Filing Jointly thresholds roughly doubled)

### Sales Tax (USA)
- No federal sales tax
- State rates range from 0% (Montana, NH, Oregon, Delaware) to ~10%+ with local additions
- VAT-like nexus rules apply — economic nexus thresholds trigger registration

### Payroll Taxes
- Social Security: 6.2% employee + 6.2% employer (up to wage base)
- Medicare: 1.45% employee + 1.45% employer (0.9% additional on high earners)
- FUTA (Federal Unemployment): 6% on first $7,000/employee (credit reduces to 0.6%)

### Key US Tax Strategies
- **Section 179 Deduction**: Immediate expensing of equipment (up to $1.16m for 2023)
- **Bonus Depreciation**: 60% (2024) of eligible property cost (phasing down)
- **Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction**: 20% deduction for pass-through income (Section 199A)
- **R&D Credit**: Credit for qualified research expenses
- **Opportunity Zones**: Defer/reduce capital gains by investing in designated areas

## UAE

### Governing Bodies & Laws
- **Federal Tax Authority (FTA)**
- **Corporate Tax Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022)**
- **VAT Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017)**

### Corporate Tax (UAE — effective June 2023)
- **0%** for taxable income up to AED 375,000
- **9%** for taxable income above AED 375,000
- **15%** for multinational enterprises (Pillar Two — revenue >AED 3.15bn)
- Free Zone companies: May qualify for 0% if meeting substance requirements

### VAT (UAE)
- Standard rate: **5%**
- Zero-rated: Exports, international transport, certain financial services, residential buildings (first supply), healthcare, education
- Exempt: Bare land, local passenger transport
- Registration threshold: AED 375,000 annual taxable supplies (mandatory)
- Voluntary registration: AED 187,500

### No Personal Income Tax in UAE
- No PAYE for individuals
- No capital gains tax (for individuals)
- No inheritance tax
- Social Security: UAE nationals only (GPSSA — 17.5% employer, 5% employee)
