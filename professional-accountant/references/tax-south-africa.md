# South Africa Tax & Compliance Reference

> Sources: South African Revenue Service (SARS) — sars.gov.za,
> Income Tax Act 58 of 1962, VAT Act 89 of 1991,
> Budget 2024, PwC SA Tax Pocket Guide 2024/25

---

## Tax Authority: SARS — sars.gov.za (eFiling: efiling.sars.gov.za)

---

## 1. Corporate Income Tax (CIT)

- **Standard rate: 27%** (reduced from 28% effective 1 April 2022)
- Small Business Corporations (SBC): sliding scale (see below)
- Public Benefit Organisations (PBO): 0% if approved
- Branches of foreign companies: 27%

### SBC Tax Rates (for qualifying companies with turnover < R20M):
| Taxable Income (ZAR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 95,750 | 0% |
| 95,751 – 365,000 | 7% of amount above 95,750 |
| 365,001 – 550,000 | R18,848 + 21% of excess |
| Above 550,000 | R57,698 + 27% of excess |

### Filing: within **12 months** after financial year-end (online via eFiling)
### Provisional Tax: 2 payments — 6 months and 12 months into tax year; 3rd optional

---

## 2. VAT

- **Standard rate: 15%** (increased from 14% in April 2018)
- **Zero-rated**: basic foodstuffs (brown bread, maize meal, samp, eggs, cooking oil,
  dried beans, lentils, milk, pilchards, rice, vegetables, fruit, edible legumes),
  exported goods, fuel (levy-paid), municipal services
- **Exempt**: residential accommodation, financial services, educational services

### Registration: mandatory when taxable supplies > R1 million per 12 months
### Voluntary registration allowed from R50,000
### Filing: monthly (category A: > R30M/month) or bi-monthly (category B)
   — due by **25th of following month** (or last business day)

---

## 3. PAYE

### Individual Tax Rates (2024/25 — tax year ends 28 February 2025):
| Taxable Income (ZAR) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 237,100 | 18% |
| 237,101 – 370,500 | R42,678 + 26% of excess |
| 370,501 – 512,800 | R77,362 + 31% of excess |
| 512,801 – 673,000 | R121,475 + 36% of excess |
| 673,001 – 857,900 | R179,147 + 39% of excess |
| 857,901 – 1,817,000 | R251,258 + 41% of excess |
| Above 1,817,000 | R644,489 + 45% of excess |

### Primary rebates (2024/25):
- Primary: R17,235
- Secondary (age ≥ 65): R9,444
- Tertiary (age ≥ 75): R3,145

### Tax thresholds (below which no tax payable):
- Under 65: R95,750
- 65–74: R148,217
- 75+: R165,689

### PAYE remittance: **7th of following month** (or last business day if < R50,000 due)
### Annual employer reconciliation (EMP501): April–May each year
### IRP5/IT3(a) certificates to employees by May 31

---

## 4. UIF — Unemployment Insurance Fund

- **Employee**: 1% of remuneration
- **Employer**: 1% of remuneration (employer bears this cost)
- **Cap**: monthly remuneration capped at R17,712 (maximum contribution = R177.12 each)
- Remit with PAYE by **7th of following month**

---

## 5. SDL — Skills Development Levy

- **Rate**: 1% of total leviable remuneration
- Companies with annual payroll < R500,000 are **exempt**
- Remit with PAYE by **7th of following month**
- 80% returned via SETA (Sector Education and Training Authority) as grants

---

## 6. Withholding Taxes (South Africa)

| Payment | Rate |
|---|---|
| Dividends (resident companies) | 20% Dividends Tax |
| Dividends (non-resident) | 20% (reduced by tax treaty) |
| Royalties (non-resident) | 15% |
| Interest (non-resident) | 15% |
| Foreign entertainers/sportspeople | 15% |
| Tax on Transfer of Property (by non-residents) | 7.5–10% |

---

## 7. Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

- Individuals: **40% of gain** included in taxable income
- Companies: **80% of gain** included in taxable income (effective rate: 21.6%)
- Annual exclusion: R40,000 per individual
- Primary residence exclusion: R2 million gain on main home

---

## 8. Key Tax Incentives

| Incentive | Detail |
|---|---|
| Section 12I | Industrial policy project investment deduction 35–55% |
| Section 11D | 150% deduction for qualifying R&D expenditure |
| EI (Employment Tax Incentive) | Reduce PAYE by up to R1,500/month per qualifying young employee |
| Special Economic Zones | 15% preferential CIT rate for qualifying companies |
| Small Business Corporations | Reduced tax rates (see above) |

---

## South Africa Tax Calendar

| Deadline | Obligation |
|---|---|
| 7th of every month | PAYE, UIF, SDL remittance (EMP201) |
| 25th of every month | VAT return (Category A: monthly) |
| Every 2 months | VAT return (Category B) |
| 6 months into tax year | First provisional tax payment |
| 12 months into tax year | Second provisional tax payment |
| Within 12 months of year-end | Annual corporate tax return (ITR14) |
| April–May | Annual employer reconciliation (EMP501) |
| 31 May | IRP5 certificates to employees |
