## 1. Corporation Tax (CT)

### Rates (from April 2023):
| Profits | Rate |
|---|---|
| ≤ £50,000 | 19% (Small Profits Rate) |
| £50,001 – £250,000 | Marginal Relief (effective rate between 19–25%) |
| > £250,000 | 25% (Main Rate) |

### Marginal Relief formula:
Tax = (Profits × 25%) − [Marginal Relief Fraction × (£250,000 − Profits)]
(Marginal Relief Fraction = 3/200)

### Filing and payment:
- **Small companies** (≤ £1.5M profit): CT600 within **12 months** of year end;
  tax payable **9 months + 1 day** after year end
- **Large companies** (> £1.5M): Quarterly Instalment Payments (QIPs)

### Key features:
- Capital Allowances: Annual Investment Allowance (AIA) — **£1 million** per year (100%)
- **Full Expensing**: 100% first-year allowance on qualifying plant and machinery
  (permanent from April 2023, Budget 2024)
- R&D Tax Relief: SMEs — 186% super-deduction or repayable credit 10%
  Large companies (RDEC) — 20% above-the-line credit
- Patent Box: 10% effective rate on profits from qualifying patents
- Group Relief: losses can be surrendered within a 75% group
- Loss carry-back: up to 3 years (for losses up to £2M)

## 2. VAT

- **Standard rate: 20%**
- **Reduced rate: 5%** (domestic energy, sanitary products, children's car seats)
- **Zero-rated**: food (most), children's clothing, books/newspapers, prescriptions, exports

### Registration: mandatory when taxable turnover exceeds **£90,000** (2024 threshold)

### Making Tax Digital (MTD for VAT):
- All VAT-registered businesses must use MTD-compatible software
- Submit returns via MTD API link — quarterly or monthly
- **Filing deadline**: 1 calendar month + 7 days after period end
- **Payment deadline**: same as filing

### VAT schemes for SMEs:
- **Flat Rate Scheme**: pay fixed % of gross turnover (varies by sector 4–14.5%)
  — available if turnover < £150,000
- **Cash Accounting**: account for VAT when paid/received (not invoiced)
  — available if turnover < £1.35M
- **Annual Accounting**: one return per year + monthly/quarterly payments

## 3. PAYE & Employer Obligations

### Income Tax Rates (2024/25):
| Income (£) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 12,570 | 0% (Personal Allowance) |
| 12,571 – 50,270 | 20% (Basic Rate) |
| 50,271 – 125,140 | 40% (Higher Rate) |
| Above 125,140 | 45% (Additional Rate) |

Note: Personal Allowance tapered by £1 for every £2 above £100,000

### National Insurance Contributions (NICs):
**Employee (Class 1):**
- £242–£967/week: 8%
- Above £967/week: 2%

**Employer (Class 1):**
- Above £175/week: **13.8%** (no cap)
- Employer NI from April 2025: **15%** (announced Budget Oct 2024)
- Secondary threshold reducing to £5,000/year from April 2025

### Employment Allowance:
- Up to **£5,000** reduction in employer NIC per year
- Not available if sole employee is also director; or if liability ≥ £100,000

### PAYE remittance:
- Monthly: **19th of following month** (22nd for electronic)
- Quarterly (if liability < £1,500/month): 19th after quarter end
- Real Time Information (RTI): submit Full Payment Submission (FPS) on or before payday

## 4. Dividends (for owner-managed companies)

### Dividend Tax (2024/25):
| Tax Band | Dividend Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Basic Rate | 8.75% |
| Higher Rate | 33.75% |
| Additional Rate | 39.35% |

**Dividend Allowance**: £500 per year (reduced from £2,000 in 2022/23)

### Optimal salary/dividend strategy:
For a director-shareholder (2024/25):
- Pay salary of £12,570 (above NI threshold of £9,100 to qualify for state pension year)
- Take remaining profit as dividends (using £500 allowance tax-free)
- Excess dividends taxed at 8.75% (basic rate) or 33.75% (higher rate)
- Company saves CT; director avoids Class 1 NIC on dividends

## 5. Capital Gains Tax (CGT) — 2024/25

### Rates (post-Budget October 2024):
| Asset | Basic Rate | Higher Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Shares / investments | 18% | 24% |
| Residential property | 18% | 24% |
| Other assets | 18% | 24% |

- **Annual Exempt Amount**: £3,000 (2024/25)
- **Business Asset Disposal Relief** (formerly Entrepreneurs' Relief):
  10% on gains up to lifetime limit of £1 million (qualifying business disposals)

## UK Tax Calendar (Companies)

| Deadline | Obligation |
|---|---|
| Monthly 19th (22nd electronic) | PAYE & NIC payment |
| On/before payday | RTI Full Payment Submission |
| 1 month + 7 days after VAT period | VAT return and payment |
| 9 months + 1 day after year-end | Corporation Tax payment (small companies) |
| 12 months after year-end | CT600 filing |
| 31 January | Self-Assessment returns and payment |
| 31 July | Self-Assessment second payment on account |
