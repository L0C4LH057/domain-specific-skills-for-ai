## 1. Company Income Tax (CIT)

**Governing Law:** Companies Income Tax Act (CITA) Cap C21 LFN 2004 (as amended)

### Rates (Finance Act 2023):
| Company Size | Annual Turnover | CIT Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Small Company | < ₦25 million | 0% (exempt) |
| Medium Company | ₦25M – ₦100M | 20% |
| Large Company | > ₦100M | 30% |

### Key rules:
- Tax is charged on **total profit** (assessable profit less reliefs and allowances)
- Minimum tax: 0.5% of gross turnover (applies when CIT result is lower)
  - Exemptions: companies in first 4 years of business, companies with >25% import capital
- **Pioneer Status** — Up to 5 years tax holiday for qualifying industries (apply to NIPC)
- **Tertiary Education Tax (TET)**: 2.5% of assessable profit (paid to TETFUND)
- **NASENI Levy**: 0.25% of profit before tax (for companies with turnover > ₦100M)

### Filing deadlines:
- Annual CIT Return: within **6 months** of company's financial year end
- Payment of balance of tax: same date as filing
- Estimated Tax (provisional): within **3 months** of year start — pay 1/3 quarterly

### Allowable deductions:
✅ Interest on loans (subject to thin capitalisation — max D/E ratio 3:1 for related parties)
✅ Rent on business premises
✅ Bad debts (specifically identified, written off)
✅ Repairs and maintenance
✅ Staff salaries and wages
✅ Depreciation (capital allowances — not accounting depreciation)

### Capital Allowances (replaces accounting depreciation for tax):
| Asset Class | Initial Allowance | Annual Allowance |
|---|---|---|
| Industrial Buildings | 15% | 10% |
| Non-Industrial Buildings | — | 10% |
| Plant & Machinery (general) | 50% | 25% |
| Motor Vehicles | 50% | 25% |
| Agricultural Equipment | 95% | — |
| Furniture & Fittings | 25% | 20% |
| Computer / IT Equipment | 66.67% | 33.33% |

## 2. Value Added Tax (VAT)

**Governing Law:** Value Added Tax Act Cap V1 LFN 2004 (as amended by Finance Acts)

### Rate:
- **Standard Rate: 7.5%** (increased from 5% by Finance Act 2019)
- VAT is charged on supply of taxable goods and services in Nigeria

### Exempt supplies (Zero-rated or exempt):
- Basic food items (unprocessed agricultural produce, cereals, flour, fish, eggs, milk)
- Medical supplies and services
- Baby products
- Educational services
- Exported goods (zero-rated — VAT charged at 0%, input VAT refundable)
- Commercial flights
- Fertilizers, agricultural and veterinary medicine

### Registration threshold:
- **₦25 million** annual taxable turnover → must register for VAT
- Voluntary registration available below threshold
- Non-resident companies providing digital services to Nigerian consumers must register

### Filing & remittance:
- **Monthly** VAT returns — due by **21st of following month**
- File on TaxProMax portal
- Penalty for late filing: ₦50,000 first month + ₦25,000 per subsequent month
- Penalty for non-remittance: **150% of VAT** not remitted + interest

### Input VAT recovery:
- Input VAT on business expenses is recoverable
- **NOT recoverable**: VAT on entertainment, motor vehicles purchased for personal use,
  assets not used in taxable supply
- Recoverable VAT is offset against output VAT; surplus is refundable (in theory —
  in practice, FIRS tends to grant credits rather than cash refunds)

## 3. PAYE — Pay As You Earn (Personal Income Tax)

**Governing Law:** Personal Income Tax Act (PITA) Cap P8 LFN 2004

### Tax bands (2024):
| Chargeable Income (annual) | Rate |
|---|---|
| First ₦300,000 | 7% |
| Next ₦300,000 | 11% |
| Next ₦500,000 | 15% |
| Next ₦500,000 | 19% |
| Next ₦1,600,000 | 21% |
| Above ₦3,200,000 | 24% |

### Allowances & reliefs:
- **Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA)**: ₦200,000 + 20% of gross income (whichever is higher)
- **Child allowance**: ₦2,500 per child (max 4 children)
- **Disability allowance**: up to ₦3,000
- **Dependent relative allowance**: ₦2,000
- **Life assurance premium**: actual premium paid
- **Employer's pension contribution**: fully deductible from employee's income
- **Employee's pension contribution (PFA)**: 8% of monthly emolument — tax-exempt

### Tax-exempt income for employees:
- Medical/dental/optical expenses reimbursed by employer
- Meal allowance up to N3,000 per day
- Transport allowance (if purely for business purposes)
- Housing benefit (partially — excess over 10% of basic salary is taxable)

### PAYE remittance:
- Deduct from employee's salary on payday
- **Remit to State IRS by 10th of the following month** (state of employee's work location)
- Annual returns (Form G) due by **31 January** each year
- Each employee needs a Tax Identification Number (TIN)

## 4. Withholding Tax (WHT)

WHT is a payment mechanism — an advance tax deducted at source by the payer
and remitted to FIRS on behalf of the recipient.

### WHT rates (key categories):
| Income Type | Corporate Recipient | Individual Recipient |
|---|---|---|
| Dividends | 10% | 10% |
| Interest | 10% | 10% |
| Rent (moveable property) | 10% | 10% |
| Rent (immoveable property) | 10% | 10% |
| Director fees | 10% | 10% |
| Management fees | 10% | 10% |
| Technical/professional fees | 5% | 5% |
| Contracts (construction/agency) | 5% | 5% |
| Commission | 10% | 5% |
| Royalties | 10% | 5% |

### Remittance:
- **Remit to FIRS within 21 days** of deduction
- File WHT return on TaxProMax
- Recipient uses WHT credit to offset their own CIT liability
- Obtain **Credit Note** from FIRS to give to recipient as evidence of deduction

## 5. Stamp Duties

**Finance Act 2020** expanded stamp duty significantly.

| Instrument | Duty |
|---|---|
| Electronic money transfers > ₦10,000 (bank to bank) | ₦50 per transaction (FIRS collects) |
| Share certificates | 1.5% of value |
| Mortgage / charge | 0.375% of loan value |
| Contract / agreement | ₦500 – ₦1,000 |
| Tenancy agreement (< 7 years) | 0.78% of annual rent |
| Tenancy agreement (7–21 years) | 3% of annual rent |
| Receipt > ₦200 | ₦1.50 |

## 6. Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

- **Rate: 10%** on chargeable gains from disposal of chargeable assets
- **Exempt**: gains from disposal of a Nigerian company's shares (Finance Act 2021 restored this exemption for shares listed on Nigerian Exchange)
- **Exempt**: compensation for loss of employment (up to ₦10M)
- CGT is separate from CIT — filed separately on Form CGT 1

---

## 7. Transfer Pricing

**Regulations:** FIRS Transfer Pricing Regulations 2018 (amended 2021)

- Applies to **related-party transactions** (companies under common ownership/control)
- Arm's length principle must be applied
- **Annual disclosure**: companies with related-party transactions must file TP Disclosure Form
- **Country-by-Country Report (CbCR)**: MNE groups with annual revenue > ₦160 billion must file
- Penalties: ₦10M–₦50M per year of non-compliance

## 8. Tax Compliance Calendar (Nigeria)

| Month | Obligation |
|---|---|
| January | PAYE Annual Return (Form G) due 31 Jan; TET payment due |
| Every month by 10th | PAYE remittance to State IRS |
| Every month by 21st | VAT return and remittance |
| Every month by 21st | WHT remittance |
| Within 3 months of year start | Estimated CIT (provisional) |
| Within 6 months of year end | Annual CIT return and payment |
| As applicable | Capital Gains Tax return |
| Annually | Transfer Pricing disclosure |

---

## 9. Tax Identification Number (TIN)

- Every company must have a TIN (obtain from FIRS via TaxProMax registration)
- Every employee must have a TIN (obtain via FIRS or JTB — Joint Tax Board)
- TIN required for: opening corporate bank accounts, government contracts,
  importing goods, registering property

## 10. Penalties & Offences Summary

| Offence | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Late CIT filing | ₦25,000 first month + ₦5,000 per subsequent month |
| Late VAT filing | ₦50,000 first month + ₦25,000 per subsequent month |
| Late PAYE filing | 10% of tax due + interest at CBN MPR + 5% |
| Failure to deduct WHT | 10% of undeducted amount + interest |
| Tax evasion | Criminal prosecution, up to 3 years imprisonment + 100% of tax evaded |
| Tax fraud | Up to 5 years imprisonment |

## 11. Tax Exemptions & Incentives Summary

| Incentive | Qualifying Criteria |
|---|---|
| Pioneer Status | Apply to NIPC; new/strategic industries; 3–5 year tax holiday |
| Free Trade Zone | Companies in NEPZA zones — CIT exempt, customs duty free |
| Small Company Exemption | Turnover < ₦25M — CIT exempt, reduced filing requirements |
| Agricultural Companies | Capital allowances at 95% initial (planting, processing) |
| Export processing incentives | Export Expansion Grant; customs duty drawback |
| Research & Development | 20% investment credit for qualifying R&D expenditure |
| Rural Investment | 15% credit for investment in rural infrastructure |
