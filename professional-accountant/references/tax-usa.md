## 1. Federal Corporate Income Tax

- **Flat rate: 21%** (since Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017)
- No surtax; applies to all C-corporations
- **Fiscal year-end**: can choose any month-end

### Key features:
- **Section 179 Expensing**: up to **$1.22 million** (2024) of equipment costs in year 1
- **Bonus Depreciation**: 60% first-year bonus depreciation (2024); phasing down 20%/year
- **R&D Tax Credit**: up to 20% of qualifying research expenses
- **Net Operating Loss (NOL)**: carry forward indefinitely; limited to 80% of taxable income
- **Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction**: for pass-through entities — up to 20%
- **GILTI** (Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income): 10.5% minimum tax on foreign profits of US MNCs
- **BEAT** (Base Erosion Anti-Abuse Tax): 10% minimum tax for large companies making deductible payments to foreign affiliates

### Filing:
- C-Corp (Form 1120): due **15th day of 4th month** after year-end (April 15 for Dec year-end)
- S-Corp (Form 1120S): due **15th day of 3rd month** (March 15)
- Partnership (Form 1065): due March 15
- Extensions available (6 months for income tax returns)

## 2. Entity Types & Tax Treatment

| Entity | Federal Tax Treatment | Self-Employment Tax |
|---|---|---|
| Sole Proprietor | Schedule C on personal return | Yes (15.3% on net profit) |
| Single-member LLC | Disregarded (Schedule C) | Yes |
| Multi-member LLC | Partnership (Form 1065) | Yes |
| S-Corporation | Pass-through (Form 1120S) | Only on salary |
| C-Corporation | Entity-level 21% CIT | No (but double taxation on dividends) |

**Strategic note**: S-Corp election saves SE tax on profits above reasonable salary.
C-Corp beneficial for high-growth companies reinvesting profits (no pass-through taxation on retained earnings).

## 3. Sales Tax (VAT equivalent)

**Critical difference from most countries: No federal sales tax in the USA.**
Sales tax is levied at **state and local level**:

| State | State Sales Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| California | 7.25% (+ local up to 10.75%) |
| Texas | 6.25% (+ local up to 8.25%) |
| New York | 4% (+ local, NYC total 8.875%) |
| Florida | 6% (+ local) |
| No sales tax | Oregon, Montana, New Hampshire, Delaware, Alaska |

**Economic Nexus (post-Wayfair 2018)**: If you sell > $100,000 or 200 transactions
into a state, you may have sales tax nexus even without physical presence.
Register and collect sales tax in each applicable state.

## 4. Payroll Taxes & Employment

### Federal payroll taxes (employer responsibilities):

| Tax | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| Social Security | 6.2% (up to $168,600 wage base 2024) | 6.2% |
| Medicare | 1.45% (no cap) | 1.45% |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% (on wages > $200K) | — |
| FUTA (Federal Unemployment) | — | 6% (on first $7,000; 5.4% credit if state FUTA paid) |

### Federal Income Tax Withholding:
- Use IRS withholding tables (Publication 15-T)
- Employees complete Form W-4 to direct withholding

### Filing deadlines:
- **Form 941** (quarterly payroll return): April 30, July 31, Oct 31, Jan 31
- **FUTA (Form 940)**: January 31 annually
- **W-2 to employees**: January 31
- **W-3 to SSA**: January 31
- **1099-NEC (contractors)**: January 31

---

## 5. 1099 vs. W-2 — Employee vs. Contractor

| W-2 Employee | 1099 Contractor |
|---|---|
| Employer pays 7.65% payroll tax | No employer payroll tax |
| Withhold income tax | No withholding obligation |
| Entitled to benefits (FMLA, etc.) | No employer benefits required |
| Issue W-2 by Jan 31 | Issue 1099-NEC (if paid ≥ $600) by Jan 31 |

**Warning**: Misclassifying employees as contractors carries significant penalties —
back payroll taxes + penalties + interest.

## 6. Individual Federal Income Tax (2024)

| Taxable Income (Single) | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $11,600 | 10% |
| $11,601 – $47,150 | 12% |
| $47,151 – $100,525 | 22% |
| $100,526 – $191,950 | 24% |
| $191,951 – $243,725 | 32% |
| $243,726 – $609,350 | 35% |
| Above $609,350 | 37% |

**Standard Deduction (2024)**: $14,600 (single); $29,200 (married filing jointly)
