## 1. HAEMATOLOGY

### Full Blood Count (FBC / CBC)
**Sample:** EDTA whole blood (purple/lavender top) | **Volume:** 3 mL

| Parameter | Male | Female | Unit | Critical |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Haemoglobin (Hb) | 13.0 – 17.0 | 12.0 – 16.0 | g/dL | <5 or >20 |
| Haematocrit (PCV/HCT) | 0.40 – 0.50 | 0.36 – 0.46 | L/L | <0.15 |
| RBC Count | 4.5 – 5.5 | 4.0 – 5.0 | x10¹²/L | — |
| MCV | 80 – 100 | 80 – 100 | fL | — |
| MCH | 27 – 33 | 27 – 33 | pg | — |
| MCHC | 31.5 – 35.0 | 31.5 – 35.0 | g/dL | — |
| WBC (Total) | 4.0 – 11.0 | 4.0 – 11.0 | x10⁹/L | <2.0 or >30 |
| Neutrophils | 40 – 75 | 40 – 75 | % | — |
| Lymphocytes | 20 – 40 | 20 – 40 | % | — |
| Monocytes | 2 – 10 | 2 – 10 | % | — |
| Eosinophils | 1 – 6 | 1 – 6 | % | — |
| Platelets | 150 – 400 | 150 – 400 | x10⁹/L | <50 or >1000 |

**Note (Nigerian context):** Haemoglobin reference ranges for Nigerians are generally similar to international standards, but chronic mild anaemia from malaria, nutritional deficiency, or sickle cell trait is common. Always note sickling status where available.

### ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate)
**Method:** Westergren | **Sample:** EDTA + 0.9% NaCl dilution
| Age/Sex | Reference Range |
|---|---|
| Males < 50 yrs | 0 – 15 mm/hr |
| Males > 50 yrs | 0 – 20 mm/hr |
| Females < 50 yrs | 0 – 20 mm/hr |
| Females > 50 yrs | 0 – 30 mm/hr |

### Blood Group & Genotype
**Sample:** EDTA blood | ABO + Rh(D) typing
- Genotypes: AA, AS, SS, AC, SC (highly relevant in Nigeria; HbS prevalence is significant)
- Report example: *"Blood Group: O Rh(D) POSITIVE | Genotype: AS"*

### Clotting Profile
**Sample:** Sodium citrate (blue top) — must be filled exactly to line
| Test | Reference Range | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| Prothrombin Time (PT) | 11 – 15 | seconds |
| INR | 0.8 – 1.2 | — |
| APTT | 25 – 38 | seconds |
| Thrombin Time (TT) | 14 – 19 | seconds |
| Fibrinogen | 2.0 – 4.0 | g/L |

**Critical:** INR > 5.0 → call clinician immediately

### Peripheral Blood Film (PBF)
Report format:
- RBC morphology: normocytic normochromic / microcytic hypochromic / macrocytic / sickle cells / target cells
- WBC differential (if abnormal morphology noted)
- Platelet estimate: adequate / reduced / increased
- Parasite: none seen / malaria parasites seen (specify P. falciparum, P. vivax if possible)

## 2. CHEMICAL PATHOLOGY (CLINICAL CHEMISTRY)

### Liver Function Tests (LFT)
**Sample:** Plain/SST (red/gold top) serum | **Volume:** 5 mL

| Test | Reference Range | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| Total Bilirubin | 3.0 – 21.0 | µmol/L |
| Direct (Conjugated) Bilirubin | 0 – 5.0 | µmol/L |
| Indirect Bilirubin | < 17.0 | µmol/L |
| ALT (SGPT) | 7 – 56 | U/L |
| AST (SGOT) | 10 – 40 | U/L |
| ALP | 44 – 147 | U/L |
| GGT | Males: 8–61, Females: 5–36 | U/L |
| Total Protein | 64 – 83 | g/L |
| Albumin | 35 – 52 | g/L |
| Globulin | 20 – 35 | g/L |

### Renal Function Tests (RFT / E/U/Cr / U&E)
**Sample:** Plain serum (red top)

| Test | Reference Range | Unit | Critical |
|---|---|---|---|
| Urea | 2.5 – 7.5 | mmol/L | >25 |
| Creatinine (Male) | 62 – 115 | µmol/L | >700 |
| Creatinine (Female) | 53 – 97 | µmol/L | >700 |
| Sodium (Na+) | 135 – 145 | mmol/L | <120 or >155 |
| Potassium (K+) | 3.5 – 5.0 | mmol/L | <2.5 or >6.5 |
| Chloride (Cl-) | 95 – 107 | mmol/L | — |
| Bicarbonate (HCO3-) | 22 – 29 | mmol/L | <10 |
| Uric Acid (Male) | 210 – 420 | µmol/L | — |
| Uric Acid (Female) | 150 – 350 | µmol/L | — |

### Blood Glucose
**Sample FBS:** Fluoride oxalate (grey top) — fasting ≥ 8 hours
**Sample RBS:** Fluoride oxalate — random

| Test | Normal | Pre-Diabetic | Diabetic | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FBS | 3.9 – 5.5 | 5.6 – 6.9 | ≥ 7.0 | mmol/L |
| RBS | < 7.8 | — | ≥ 11.1 | mmol/L |
| PPBS (2hr) | < 7.8 | 7.8 – 11.0 | ≥ 11.1 | mmol/L |
| HbA1c | < 5.7% normal | 5.7–6.4% | ≥ 6.5% | % |

**Critical:** < 2.5 mmol/L or > 25 mmol/L → call ward immediately

### Lipid Profile
**Sample:** Plain serum | Fasting ≥ 12 hours required

| Parameter | Desirable | Borderline High | High Risk | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total Cholesterol | < 5.2 | 5.2 – 6.2 | > 6.2 | mmol/L |
| LDL Cholesterol | < 3.4 | 3.4 – 4.1 | > 4.1 | mmol/L |
| HDL Cholesterol | > 1.0 (M), > 1.3 (F) | — | < 1.0 | mmol/L |
| Triglycerides | < 1.7 | 1.7 – 2.3 | > 2.3 | mmol/L |

### Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)
**Sample:** Plain serum

| Test | Reference Range | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| TSH | 0.4 – 4.0 | mIU/L |
| Free T4 (fT4) | 9.0 – 24.0 | pmol/L |
| Free T3 (fT3) | 3.5 – 7.8 | pmol/L |

### PSA (Prostate Specific Antigen)
**Sample:** Plain serum | Males only
- Age 40–49: < 2.5 ng/mL
- Age 50–59: < 3.5 ng/mL
- Age 60–69: < 4.5 ng/mL
- Age 70+: < 6.5 ng/mL

### CRP (C-Reactive Protein)
- Normal: < 5.0 mg/L
- Moderate inflammation: 5 – 40 mg/L
- Severe infection/inflammation: > 40 mg/L

## 3. MICROBIOLOGY & PARASITOLOGY

### Malaria Parasite (MP/BS)
**Sample:** EDTA blood or finger-prick capillary blood | **Method:** Thick + thin film with Giemsa stain, or RDT (Rapid Diagnostic Test)

**Report format:**
```
Malaria Parasite: SEEN / NOT SEEN
Species: Plasmodium falciparum (most common in Nigeria)
Parasitaemia: + / ++ / +++ / ++++ (semi-quantitative)
Method: Giemsa stained thick film
```
**Note:** P. falciparum accounts for >90% of malaria in Nigeria. Always report species if identifiable.

### WIDAL Test (Typhoid Fever Serology)
**Sample:** Plain serum | **Method:** Tube agglutination (Widal)
| Antigen | Significant Titre (Single Sample) |
|---|---|
| S. Typhi H (flagellar) | ≥ 1:160 |
| S. Typhi O (somatic) | ≥ 1:160 |
| S. Paratyphi AH | ≥ 1:80 |
| S. Paratyphi BH | ≥ 1:80 |

**Important note for Nigerian context:** WIDAL results must be interpreted alongside clinical picture — endemic area means baseline titres may be elevated. A fourfold rise in paired sera (10–14 days apart) is more diagnostic. Many labs note: *"Results should be interpreted in the light of clinical findings."*

### Urine M/C/S (Microscopy, Culture & Sensitivity)
**Sample:** Midstream clean-catch urine (MSU) in sterile universal container
**Report structure:**
1. **Macroscopy:** Colour, turbidity
2. **Microscopy:** Pus cells/HPF, RBCs/HPF, epithelial cells, casts, crystals
3. **Culture:** Growth after 18–24 hrs — organism identified
4. **Sensitivity:** Disc diffusion (Kirby-Bauer) — list antibiotics as S/I/R

**Significant bacteriuria:** ≥ 10⁵ CFU/mL (midstream)

**Commonly isolated organisms in Nigeria:**
- E. coli, Klebsiella, Pseudomonas, Staphylococcus saprophyticus, Enterococcus

### HVS (High Vaginal Swab) / Cervical Swab
**Report:** Direct microscopy (wet prep + Gram stain), culture & sensitivity
- Note presence of: clue cells (BV), yeast/hyphae (Candida), Trichomonas vaginalis
- Culture: Candida spp., GBS, Neisseria gonorrhoeae, Staphylococcus spp.

### Sputum AFB (Acid Fast Bacilli — TB screening)
**Method:** ZN (Ziehl-Neelsen) stain | Collect 3 early morning sputum samples
| Grading | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Negative | No AFB seen in 300 fields |
| Scanty | 1–9 AFB/100 fields |
| 1+ | 10–99 AFB/100 fields |
| 2+ | 1–10 AFB/field |
| 3+ | > 10 AFB/field |

**Nigerian context:** Nigeria is a high TB burden country. Positive AFB must be reported immediately and specimen forwarded to reference lab for culture and DST (Drug Susceptibility Testing).

## 4. SEROLOGY / IMMUNOLOGY

### HIV Testing (Nigerian NACA Algorithm)
**Mandatory 3-test algorithm per NACA/FMOH guidelines:**
1. **Determine™** (screening test)
2. **Unigold™** (confirmatory if Determine positive)
3. **Stat-Pak™** (tiebreaker if Determine and Unigold discordant)

**Result interpretation:**
- Both positive → HIV Positive
- Both negative → HIV Negative
- Discordant → use tiebreaker; if still discordant → retest in 2–4 weeks
- Report: *"HIV Antibody: REACTIVE / NON-REACTIVE (Method: Rapid)"*

### HBsAg (Hepatitis B Surface Antigen)
**Method:** ELISA or Rapid test | **Sample:** Serum
- Reactive = HBV infection present (refer for further testing: HBeAg, HBV DNA, LFT)
- Non-Reactive = No current HBV detected

### HCV Antibody
**Method:** Rapid test or ELISA
- Reactive = Exposure to HCV (confirm with PCR/viral load)

### VDRL / RPR (Syphilis)
**Method:** Qualitative + quantitative titres
- Reactive VDRL → confirm with TPHA or FTA-Abs
- Titre report: e.g., *"RPR: REACTIVE (1:8)"*

### Pregnancy Test (βhCG)
**Sample:** Urine or serum
- Positive = Pregnant (present from ~7–10 days post-implantation)
- Report: *"Urine Pregnancy Test: POSITIVE / NEGATIVE"*

### ASO Titre (Anti-Streptolysin O)
- Positive: > 200 IU/mL in adults; > 400 IU/mL in children (high activity)
- Indicates recent Group A Streptococcal infection

### Rheumatoid Factor (RF)
- Negative: < 14 IU/mL
- Positive: ≥ 14 IU/mL (report titre)

## 5. URINALYSIS

### Dipstick Analysis
**Sample:** Midstream urine, fresh (within 2 hours)

| Parameter | Normal Finding |
|---|---|
| Colour | Pale yellow to amber |
| Appearance | Clear |
| pH | 4.5 – 8.0 |
| Specific Gravity | 1.003 – 1.030 |
| Protein | Negative |
| Glucose | Negative |
| Blood | Negative |
| Leucocytes | Negative |
| Nitrites | Negative |
| Ketones | Negative |
| Bilirubin | Negative |
| Urobilinogen | Normal |

### Microscopy (per High Power Field — HPF)
| Finding | Normal | Abnormal |
|---|---|---|
| Pus Cells (WBCs) | 0–5/HPF | >10/HPF = pyuria |
| RBCs | 0–3/HPF | >5/HPF = haematuria |
| Epithelial Cells | Few | >5/HPF = contamination |
| Casts | None/rare hyaline | Granular/cellular = pathological |
| Crystals | Variable | Some pathological (oxalate, urate, cystine) |

## 6. SAMPLE TYPES & COLLECTION TUBES

| Tube Colour | Additive | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Red (plain) | None | Serology, Chemistry (serum) |
| Gold/SST | Gel separator | Chemistry (preferred for serum) |
| Purple/Lavender | EDTA | Haematology (FBC, blood group) |
| Blue | Sodium citrate | Coagulation studies — fill to line exactly |
| Grey | Sodium fluoride/K oxalate | Glucose, lactate |
| Green | Lithium heparin | Plasma chemistry, emergency |
| Yellow | ACD | Blood culture, flow cytometry |

**Order of draw (to prevent cross-contamination):**
Blood cultures → Blue → Red/Gold → Green → Purple → Grey

**Power/NEPA interruption protocol:** Always note time of power loss in the logbook. Samples in analysers should be re-run after power is restored and QC verified.
